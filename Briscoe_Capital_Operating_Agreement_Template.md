# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF BRISCOE CAPITAL, LLC
### A Texas Limited Liability Company

> **How to use this document:** This is an illustrative template built around your specific setup — three family members, two managing/two non-managing roles, future acquisition vehicles underneath the holding company, and SBA lending in the picture. Every bracketed item `[LIKE THIS]` is a placeholder you need to fill in or decide on. This is not a finished legal document — have an attorney review it before anyone signs, especially Article V (decision-making authority) and Article X (buyout mechanics), which are the two places family LLCs most often run into trouble later.

## ARTICLE I — FORMATION

**1.1 Formation.** Briscoe Capital, LLC (the "Company") was formed as a Texas limited liability company upon the filing of its Certificate of Formation with the Texas Secretary of State on `[DATE]`.

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is Briscoe Capital, LLC.

**1.3 Registered Agent and Office.** The Company's registered agent is `[NAME]`, located at `[ADDRESS]`. The principal office of the Company is `[ADDRESS]`.

**1.4 Term.** The Company shall continue perpetually unless dissolved in accordance with Article XIII.

## ARTICLE II — PURPOSE

**2.1 Purpose.** The Company is organized to acquire, hold, and manage equity interests in one or more operating subsidiaries engaged in home services and related small business sectors, and to engage in any other lawful business activity as the Members may determine.

**2.2 Subsidiary Structure.** The Members anticipate that each business acquired by the Company will be held through a separate, single-purpose subsidiary entity ("Acquisition Subsidiary") wholly or majority owned by the Company, in order to isolate liability between operating businesses.

## ARTICLE III — MEMBERS AND MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

**3.1 Initial Members.** The Members of the Company, their initial capital contributions, and their Membership Interests are set forth on **Exhibit A**.

**3.2 Classes of Membership.**
- **Managing Members:** `[DYLAN BRISCOE]` and `[BRADLEY BRISCOE]`, who hold management authority as set forth in Article V.
- **Non-Managing Member:** `[FATHER'S NAME]`, who holds an economic interest and consent rights over Major Decisions (Section 5.5) but no day-to-day management authority.

**3.3 No Personal Liability.** Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement or by separate written guaranty, no Member shall be personally liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a Member.

**3.4 Admission of New Members.** No person may be admitted as a new Member without the unanimous written consent of all existing Members.

---

## ARTICLE IV — CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Initial Contributions.** Each Member's initial capital contribution is set forth on Exhibit A. Contributions shall be made in cash, by wire or check, with documentation retained for lender and tax purposes.

**4.2 Additional Capital Contributions.** If the Managing Members determine that additional capital is needed (e.g., for a new acquisition's equity injection requirement), they shall deliver written notice to all Members specifying the amount needed and each Member's pro rata share based on Membership Interest. Each Member shall have `[15]` days to fund their share.

**4.3 Failure to Contribute.** If a Member fails to fund a properly noticed capital call, the other Members may (a) advance the shortfall as a loan to the non-contributing Member bearing interest at `[RATE]`, or (b) treat the failure as a dilution event, recalculating Membership Interests to reflect the actual capital contributed by each Member.

**4.4 No Interest on Contributions.** No Member shall be entitled to interest on capital contributions.

**4.5 Capital Accounts.** The Company shall maintain a capital account for each Member in accordance with standard partnership tax accounting principles.

---

## ARTICLE V — MANAGEMENT

**5.1 Manager-Managed.** The Company shall be manager-managed. Management authority is vested in the Managing Members identified in Section 3.2.

**5.2 Ordinary Course Authority.** Either Managing Member, acting alone, may bind the Company in matters in the ordinary course of business, including day-to-day operations of the Company and its Acquisition Subsidiaries, up to `[$ AMOUNT]` in any single transaction.

**5.3 Joint Managing Member Approval.** The following actions require the written approval of **both** Managing Members:
- Entering into any contract or obligation exceeding `[$ AMOUNT]`
- Hiring or terminating senior management at any Acquisition Subsidiary
- Approving the annual budget for any Acquisition Subsidiary

**5.4 Non-Managing Member Role.** The Non-Managing Member shall have the right to receive financial reports under Article VIII and to consent to Major Decisions under Section 5.5, but shall not participate in day-to-day management or bind the Company to third parties.

**5.5 Major Decisions — Unanimous Consent of All Members Required.** Notwithstanding Sections 5.2–5.3, the following require the unanimous written consent of **all** Members, including the Non-Managing Member:
- Acquiring a new business or forming a new Acquisition Subsidiary
- Incurring debt secured by an SBA guaranty, or any debt exceeding `[$ AMOUNT]`
- Selling, transferring, or encumbering a material asset of the Company
- Admitting a new Member or issuing new Membership Interests
- Amending this Agreement
- Dissolving the Company

## ARTICLE VI — MEETINGS AND VOTING

**6.1 Voting Rights.** Except as otherwise stated, voting power is proportional to each Member's Membership Interest percentage.

**6.2 Meetings.** Members shall meet at least `[ANNUALLY/QUARTERLY]`, with `[7]` days' written notice. Meetings may be held by phone or video conference.

**6.3 Action Without Meeting.** Any action requiring Member approval may be taken without a meeting by written consent signed by the Members holding the requisite voting threshold for that action.

---

## ARTICLE VII — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**7.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses.** Profits and losses shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their Membership Interests.

**7.2 Tax Distributions.** The Managing Members shall use reasonable efforts to distribute, at least annually, an amount sufficient for each Member to pay estimated income tax attributable to the Company's allocated income, to the extent cash is available and not restricted by lender covenants.

**7.3 Discretionary Distributions.** Beyond tax distributions, the timing and amount of further distributions is within the discretion of the Managing Members, subject to maintaining adequate working capital and complying with any restrictions imposed by SBA loan documents at the Acquisition Subsidiary level.

**7.4 Lender Restrictions Control.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, no distribution shall be made if prohibited by, or in violation of, any covenant in an SBA-guaranteed loan agreement binding the Company or an Acquisition Subsidiary.

## ARTICLE IX — TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

**9.1 General Restriction.** No Member may sell, assign, pledge, or otherwise transfer all or any part of their Membership Interest without the prior written consent of the other Members, except as set forth below.

**9.2 Right of First Refusal.** If a Member receives a bona fide third-party offer to purchase their interest, they must first offer it to the other Members on the same terms. The other Members have `[30]` days to accept.

**9.3 Permitted Transfers.** Transfers to a revocable living trust for estate planning purposes, or to a Member's spouse or lineal descendants, are permitted without triggering the right of first refusal, provided the transferee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.

## ARTICLE X — WITHDRAWAL AND BUYOUT

**10.1 Voluntary Withdrawal.** A Member may withdraw from the Company by giving `[90]` days' written notice. Withdrawal does not dissolve the Company.

**10.2 Triggering Events.** A Member's interest becomes subject to mandatory buyout upon: death, permanent incapacity, bankruptcy, divorce resulting in a court awarding any portion of the interest to a non-Member, or removal of a Managing Member for cause (defined as fraud, gross negligence, or material breach of this Agreement).

**10.4 Payment Terms.** The buyout amount shall be paid `[CHOOSE ONE: in a lump sum within 90 days, or via a promissory note with a [3–5] year term at [RATE]% interest, given that most Company capital will be illiquid and tied up in Acquisition Subsidiary equity]`.

## ARTICLE XI — LENDER AND GUARANTY MATTERS

**11.1 Personal Guarantees.** Any Member holding 20% or more of the Membership Interests in the Company or an Acquisition Subsidiary, or any Member otherwise required by a lender, shall execute a personal guaranty in connection with SBA-guaranteed or other financing as requested by the lender.

**11.2 Compliance with Loan Covenants.** The Company and each Acquisition Subsidiary shall comply with all affirmative and negative covenants in any SBA loan documents, including restrictions on additional debt, distributions, and changes in ownership without lender consent.

**11.3 Ownership Certification.** Each Member certifies they are a United States citizen or lawful permanent resident, and shall promptly notify the other Members and any relevant lender of any change in that status.

---

## ARTICLE XII — INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

**12.1 Limitation of Liability.** No Managing Member shall be liable to the Company or other Members for actions taken in good faith on behalf of the Company, except for fraud, gross negligence, or willful misconduct.

**12.2 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify each Managing Member against losses, claims, and expenses incurred in connection with Company business, to the fullest extent permitted by Texas law.

---

## ARTICLE XIII — DISSOLUTION

**13.1 Dissolution Events.** The Company shall dissolve upon: (a) unanimous written consent of all Members, (b) entry of a judicial decree of dissolution, or (c) any other event requiring dissolution under Texas law.

**13.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Managing Members (or a Member appointed by majority vote) shall wind up the Company's affairs, liquidate assets, pay creditors, and distribute remaining proceeds to Members in proportion to their capital account balances.

### Notes for your attorney review pass
1. **Article V, Section 5.5** — the Major Decisions list is the single most important section in a family LLC. Make sure it actually reflects how the three of you intend to operate, not a generic list.
2. **Article X** — pick an actual valuation method now, while everyone agrees and there's no money on the line. This is the clause families regret leaving vague.
3. **Article XI** — once you're attaching this to a live SBA deal, your lender's counsel will want to see this language and may request specific edits. Don't be surprised if it gets marked up at that stage.
4. **Exhibit A percentages** — lock these in based on actual contributed capital, with bank records to back it up, before any of you sign.
